# General Counsel

Not a primary buyer. Economic buyer + risk sponsor in regulated + post-breach situations. Owns the Item 1.05 materiality call. Owns the BAA / contract language for healthcare. Owns the litigation posture after a breach. GC is the unlock for multi-threading in healthcare + finserv + BigLaw. Example: Megan Marshall at [[../../10_Accounts/Medusind]] is a GC angle post-settlement.

## What they actually do day-to-day

- Sign off on regulatory disclosures (SEC 8-K Item 1.05, HIPAA breach notifications, state AG notifications).
- Manage outside counsel for cyber incidents.
- Approve + negotiate cyber vendor contracts + DPAs.
- Own board-level legal risk briefings, including cyber.
- Coordinate with CFO + CISO on E&O + D&O + cyber insurance renewals.

## Core pains (in their language)

- "If we have an incident, who decides materiality and how do we defend that decision?"
- "The class-action lawyers take a year to sue. I have four business days to disclose."
- "My cyber insurer wants evidence of program maturity. The CISO sends a PDF the underwriter doesn't trust."
- "Settlement terms often require ongoing security program reporting. I need a way to produce that."

## What they DON'T want to hear

- Technical jargon. GCs are lawyers, not engineers. Speak in governance + risk + disclosure language.
- "This replaces your existing tools." They don't care about the tools.
- Sales urgency. GCs respond to regulatory urgency, not quota urgency.

## Language that lands

- "Defensible materiality trail."
- "Single source of governance evidence for the board, the insurer, and the regulator."
- "Onyxia documents the continuous risk posture. That's the language settlement terms expect."
- Post-breach: "Your CISO is heads-down on the technical side. We give your team and your insurer a clean narrative of program maturity moving forward."

## Objections you will hear

- "This is the CISO's call, not mine." Redirect: "Absolutely. This matters for you because [8-K materiality / breach notification / cyber insurance defensibility]."
- "We have outside counsel managing cyber." Acknowledge. Position Onyxia as the source document for that counsel, not a replacement.
- "Send me something in writing I can share with [CISO / CFO / Board]." Excellent signal. Deliver a 2-page business justification. See [[../methodology/objections]] #9.

## CTA preferences

- 2-page business justification doc sent for board + CFO pass-along.
- 20-minute call with Sivan framed as peer CISO + GC risk conversation.
- Joint call with their CISO.

## Watch signals

- Settlement paid or finalized → [[../triggers/settlement-event]].
- 8-K Item 1.05 filed by the GC's company → [[../triggers/breach-event]] + [[../triggers/10k-8k-filing]].
- New GC hire at a post-incident company → [[../triggers/exec-hire]].
- Class-action filing against their company.

## Accounts where GC is a multi-thread angle

- [[../../10_Accounts/Medusind]] — [[Megan Marshall]], post-settlement GC.

## When NOT to lead with GC

- BigLaw. GC at BigLaw is the firm's internal legal function. Buying motion is non-responsive per [[../playbook/closed-lost-patterns]]. Don't burn GC credits here.
- Pre-breach, pre-regulatory shops. GC has no wedge. Lead with CISO.
