# SecurityScorecard

## What they sell

External security ratings platform and third-party risk management. A-F letter-grade rating for any company based on externally observable signals (DNS health, patching cadence, endpoint exposure, leaked credentials). 12 million companies continuously rated. Customer base of 3,300+ organizations including 70 percent of the Fortune 100. Recognized by CISA. March 2026 launch of TITAN AI, positioned as threat-informed TPRM.

## Where they win (vs Onyxia)

- **External-attack-surface + TPRM buyers.** Security leaders who need a continuously monitored view of their vendors' external security posture.
- **Boards that want a simple letter grade.** The A-F grading system is a strong artifact for non-technical governance conversations.
- **Regulated industries with large vendor populations.** Financial services, healthcare, government. Vendor-risk-heavy mandates.

Buyer profile: Head of TPRM, CISO at a vendor-heavy enterprise, or the Vendor Risk function inside a financial-services buyer.

## Where Onyxia wins (vs them)

SecurityScorecard sees the outside of a company. Onyxia measures the inside of the customer's own program. Different angle.

Honest differentiation:
- Onyxia pulls internal data from the customer's security stack (per [[../product/features]] integrations: CrowdStrike, Sentinel, Wiz, Okta, Entra ID, Duo, Qualys, Tenable, Rapid7, KnowBe4, Proofpoint, Mimecast, Intune, Jamf, Tanium, Orca). SecurityScorecard uses externally observable signals only.
- Onyxia's board reporting covers internal program performance against NIST CSF, HITRUST, HIPAA, SOC 2, PCI, FedRAMP (per [[../product/features]] Framework Compliance Dashboard). SecurityScorecard's board artifact is an external rating.
- SecurityScorecard's TPRM depth is beyond Onyxia's scope. Honest.

These products can co-exist. Most enterprise CISOs use an external rating tool alongside an internal program tool.

Vault is silent on any closed-won deal where Onyxia displaced SecurityScorecard. Not a natural displacement.

## Pricing signal

Enterprise-licensed, custom-quoted. Typical enterprise deployments run six figures per year depending on number of monitored third parties. Not publicly listed.

## Known customer base

3,300+ organizations, 70 percent of the Fortune 100, 180+ government agencies. No specific logo overlap documented with Onyxia's closed-won corpus. Probable overlap in any large healthcare or financial-services buyer, but not captured in vault notes.

## Onyxia's angle when this competitor is in the deal

Do not position as a TPRM replacement. SecurityScorecard is an outside-in view. Onyxia is an inside-out program view. Buyers routinely own both.

Script per [[../methodology/objections]] objection #7 calibration: "SecurityScorecard gives you an external score of yourself and your vendors. Onyxia gives you the internal program view across what your own tools are actually delivering. Both-and, not either-or. 15 minutes to see the overlap."

If the prospect's CISO has a TPRM-only mandate and no internal program-measurement gap, disqualify. Wrong angle.
