# LogicGate

## What they sell

Risk Cloud, a no-code GRC platform where customers configure their own risk, compliance, and audit workflows inside a pre-built shell. Applications include ERM, TPRM, IT & Security Risk, Policy Management, Compliance, and Controls. Named a Leader by G2 for the 27th consecutive quarter and a Leader in the Gartner Magic Quadrant for GRC Tools.

## Where they win (vs Onyxia)

- **Mid-market GRC buyers who want configurability without a big GRC engineering team.** LogicGate's no-code builder lets lean GRC teams stand up their own workflows.
- **Multi-application programs.** Customers want audit, policy, TPRM, ERM in one place with workflow automation underneath.
- **Strong G2 / customer-review reputation.** Known for implementation support and customer responsiveness.

Buyer profile: GRC Director or Head of Compliance at a mid-market or mid-enterprise company standing up or consolidating a GRC program.

## Where Onyxia wins (vs them)

LogicGate is a workflow platform; Onyxia is a measurement and reporting layer. Different jobs.

Honest differentiation:
- Onyxia pulls live data from the security stack (SIEM, EDR, IAM, cloud, training) via [[../product/features]] integration catalog. LogicGate is a workflow and forms platform on top of its own data model.
- Onyxia's framework switch (per [[../product/features]] Framework Compliance Dashboard) remaps the dashboard to NIST CSF, HITRUST, SOC 2, PCI, HIPAA, FedRAMP in one click. LogicGate customers typically configure each framework as a separate application.
- LogicGate has depth on ERM and policy that Onyxia does not cover.

LogicGate review patterns note a learning curve on initial setup and advanced reporting. For a CISO who just wants the board view without standing up a workflow, Onyxia is the faster path. For a GRC function building a program, LogicGate is the better fit.

Vault is silent on any closed-won deal where Onyxia displaced LogicGate. No Onyxia positioning language addresses LogicGate specifically.

## Pricing signal

LogicGate's pricing model is based on Applications plus Power User licenses. Standard and External users are included. Public list pricing is not disclosed; Vendr and SoftwareAdvice data suggest typical mid-market deals in the $40K-$150K per year range depending on application count.

## Known customer base

Mid-market and mid-enterprise GRC teams. No specific logo overlap documented with Onyxia's closed-won corpus.

## Onyxia's angle when this competitor is in the deal

Position as the board view and measurement layer that sits above LogicGate's workflow.

Script per [[../methodology/objections]] objection #7: "LogicGate runs your GRC workflow. We don't. We produce the board-level view of program performance pulled from your actual tooling, not the workflow system. Usually customers run both."

If the prospect is mid-implementation on LogicGate and has no board-reporting gap, defer the conversation. Time frame is not right (see [[../methodology/objections]] objection #2).
