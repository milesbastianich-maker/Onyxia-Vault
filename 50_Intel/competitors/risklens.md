# RiskLens

## What they sell

Cyber risk quantification platform built on the FAIR (Factor Analysis of Information Risk) standard. Translates technical cyber risk scenarios into financial dollar-value loss estimates and loss exceedance curves for board-level decisions. Pioneered the commercial FAIR implementation. Acquired by Safe Security in 2023 and operates as part of that broader platform. Customer base spans hundreds of large enterprises across healthcare, financial services, and technology; recognized as a Fortune 1000 risk-team tool.

## Where they win (vs Onyxia)

- **Boards and audit committees asking for dollar-loss exposure.** FAIR is the recognized standard for financial quantification of cyber risk.
- **Enterprise Risk functions integrating cyber with ORM.** CROs who want cyber risk expressed in the same units as credit, market, or operational risk.
- **Regulated financial services.** Large banks and insurers with risk-quantification programs.

Buyer profile: Chief Risk Officer, Head of Operational Risk, or a quantitatively-minded CISO at a large bank or insurer.

## Where Onyxia wins (vs them)

Different mandate. RiskLens is cyber risk quantification. Onyxia is security program measurement and board reporting.

Honest differentiation:
- RiskLens produces dollar-loss estimates per scenario via FAIR modeling. Onyxia does not. Per [[../product/features]], OnyxAI provides program trend predictions, SLA optimization, and risk-reduction recommendations. Those are operational, not actuarial.
- Onyxia pulls live data from the security stack (CrowdStrike, Wiz, Okta, Tenable, etc.) for operational KPIs. RiskLens is a scenario-modeling tool, not an operational data fabric.
- Onyxia covers framework compliance (NIST CSF, HITRUST, HIPAA, SOC 2, PCI, FedRAMP). RiskLens does not position on framework dashboarding.

Co-existence is natural. A CRO can run RiskLens for scenario-based loss modeling while the CISO runs Onyxia for operational program measurement. Different artifacts to different audiences.

Vault is silent on any closed-won deal where Onyxia displaced RiskLens. Onyxia positioning does not address cyber risk quantification explicitly.

## Pricing signal

Not publicly disclosed. Enterprise FAIR platform deployments historically run six figures per year. Safe Security's integrated platform pricing is similarly custom-quoted.

## Known customer base

Hundreds of Fortune 1000 enterprises. Strong positioning in banking, insurance, healthcare, and technology. No logo overlap documented with Onyxia's closed-won corpus.

## Onyxia's angle when this competitor is in the deal

Never try to displace RiskLens on its own turf. Different buyer, different artifact.

Script per [[../methodology/objections]] objection #1: "RiskLens quantifies risk scenarios in dollars for the board. We measure the security program operationally across your stack. If the CISO also needs the ongoing program view, Onyxia fits alongside RiskLens, not against it."

If the prospect's only mandate is risk quantification for the board, disqualify. Onyxia positioning does not address this dimension explicitly, and inventing a FAIR capability would violate [[../../00_Index/CLAUDE.md]] product-reference rules.
