# ServiceNow IRM

## What they sell

Integrated Risk Management built on the ServiceNow Now Platform. Modules include Risk Management, Policy and Compliance, Vendor Risk Management, Business Continuity, Audit Management, Operational Resilience. ServiceNow IRM is the system of workflows for 60 percent of the Fortune 500 as of 2026. Q1 2026 release adds control-objective change management with draft-and-approve workflow. In April 2026, TrustCloud launched a native AI continuous control monitoring app on the ServiceNow Store that syncs directly to IRM, SecOps, CMDB, and the AI Control Tower.

## Where they win (vs Onyxia)

- **ServiceNow-native enterprises.** Any company that already runs ServiceNow for ITSM will default to ServiceNow IRM for GRC. That is the incumbency advantage.
- **Workflow depth across GRC, audit, resilience, and ITSM.** Nothing else sits inside the same platform as change management, incident, and CMDB.
- **Fortune 500 footprint.** Well-established with large enterprise buyers and strong discounting for existing ServiceNow customers (reports of 60-80 percent off list for GRC/IRM modules for Fortune 500 ServiceNow accounts).

Buyer profile: CIO or Head of GRC at an enterprise already running ServiceNow ITSM.

## Where Onyxia wins (vs them)

Per [[../product/positioning]] differentiation table:

| ServiceNow IRM | Onyxia |
|---|---|
| Compliance workflow + policy tracking | Automated framework dashboard without the GRC workflow overhead |

Honest differentiation:
- Onyxia is faster to value for the CISO board-view use case. ServiceNow IRM requires meaningful platform engineering and workflow configuration.
- Onyxia's CPI Library (per [[../product/features]]) is pre-built against NIST CSF 2.0, HITRUST, SOC 2, PCI, HIPAA, FedRAMP. ServiceNow IRM requires that to be configured.
- The CMDB tie-in is ServiceNow's advantage, not Onyxia's. Honest.

[[../methodology/objections]] lists ServiceNow IRM as a named example in objection #1 ("just rolled out ServiceNow IRM") and #7.

Vault is silent on any closed-won deal where Onyxia displaced ServiceNow IRM. Given IRM's F500 footprint, a plausible co-existence pattern is Onyxia for CISO board reporting while ServiceNow holds the GRC workflow.

## Pricing signal

Custom enterprise contracts. Fortune 500 buyers can negotiate 60-80 percent off list for GRC/IRM modules based on 2026 licensing guides. Typical mid-enterprise deployments are multi-hundred-thousand per year with implementation services on top.

## Known customer base

60 percent of the Fortune 500 run ServiceNow somewhere; IRM adoption inside that base is the upsell motion they focus on. No specific logo overlap documented with Onyxia's closed-won corpus.

## Onyxia's angle when this competitor is in the deal

Never propose a rip-and-replace of ServiceNow IRM. Position as the security-program layer above it.

Script per [[../methodology/objections]] objection #7: "We don't replace ServiceNow IRM. We pull from it. ServiceNow holds the workflow. Onyxia holds the board view, the CPI trend predictions, and the framework-to-framework switch. Most of our customers run both."

If the prospect's security team reports into the CIO through ServiceNow governance, multi-thread to the CISO (per [[../playbook/closed-won-patterns]] multi-thread rule). The CISO often has no dedicated measurement layer even when IRM is deployed.
