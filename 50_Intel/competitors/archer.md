# Archer

## What they sell

Integrated risk management suite covering operational risk, IT and security risk, third-party governance, regulatory compliance, audit, business resiliency, and public-sector risk. Spun out from RSA in 2020. Long-established in large financial services and government buyers. Archer is the archetype of the legacy GRC platform: broad, deep, customizable, and heavy to implement.

## Where they win (vs Onyxia)

- **Large financial services and government.** Banks, insurers, federal agencies with existing Archer deployments measured in seven figures per year.
- **Deep IRM workflow breadth.** One platform for audit, ORM, IT risk, TPRM, compliance, and resiliency. Nothing else covers that surface in one SKU.
- **Customization.** Archer is highly configurable for firms with dedicated GRC engineering teams.

Buyer profile: Chief Risk Officer or Head of GRC with budget and a GRC engineering function.

## Where Onyxia wins (vs them)

Per [[../product/positioning]] differentiation table:

| Archer | Onyxia |
|---|---|
| Compliance workflow + policy tracking | Automated framework dashboard without the GRC workflow overhead |

Archer is powerful but implementation-heavy. Review patterns on Gartner / SoftwareAdvice flag the UX as dated compared to newer entrants. Onyxia is positioned above the GRC workflow, not as a workflow replacement.

Honest differentiation:
- Onyxia does not do ORM, audit, or resiliency workflow. If that is the buyer's mandate, Archer or ServiceNow IRM is the better fit.
- Onyxia produces the board-ready program view that Archer typically requires custom reporting to produce.

[[../methodology/objections]] lists Archer as a named example in objection #1 and #7. Miles has heard "We bought Archer" in the field.

Vault is silent on any closed-won deal where Onyxia displaced Archer. The closed-lost corpus has one loss tagged "Chose competing service" (Allied World), not Archer. See [[../playbook/closed-lost-patterns]].

## Pricing signal

Not publicly listed. Enterprise Archer deployments commonly run $75K-300K+ per year by module, plus implementation services that can multiply the year-one cost. Multi-year enterprise contracts at large FSI customers run into seven figures.

## Known customer base

Heavy footprint in banking, insurance, federal government, utilities. No direct logo overlap with Onyxia's closed-won corpus. Onyxia's critical-infrastructure wins (Port Authority, Summit Utilities, OCTA) did not displace Archer per available notes.

## Onyxia's angle when this competitor is in the deal

Position as the layer above Archer, not a replacement.

Script per [[../methodology/objections]] objection #1: "We don't replace Archer. We pull from it. Archer is your workflow system of record. Onyxia is the board view and program optimization layer on top. Most of our customers run both."

If the prospect is mid-cycle on an Archer rollout, multi-thread to the CISO (per [[../playbook/closed-won-patterns]] multi-thread rule) rather than competing with the GRC program owner.
