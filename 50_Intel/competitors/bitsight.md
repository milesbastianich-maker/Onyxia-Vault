# Bitsight

## What they sell

Cyber risk intelligence platform unifying external attack surface management, cyber threat intelligence, and third-party risk management on one validated data model. 3,500+ customers, 65,000 organizations active on the platform, 38 percent of the Fortune 500, 4 of top 5 investment banks, 180+ government agencies. 60,000+ pre-populated vendor assessments. Closest market peer to SecurityScorecard.

## Where they win (vs Onyxia)

- **TPRM and EASM combined.** Bitsight's unification of external attack surface management plus TPRM plus threat intelligence is distinct from single-category TPRM tools.
- **Financial services and government.** Heavy footprint with investment banks and federal agencies.
- **Vendor-assessment breadth.** 60,000+ pre-populated assessments reduce questionnaire burden for TPRM teams.
- **Framework Intelligence (AI-powered framework mapping) for TPRM.** Automates mapping of external exposure data to frameworks.

Buyer profile: CISO or Head of TPRM at a vendor-heavy financial services, government, or critical-infrastructure enterprise.

## Where Onyxia wins (vs them)

Same dynamic as SecurityScorecard. Bitsight looks at the outside of companies. Onyxia measures the inside of the customer's own program.

Honest differentiation:
- Onyxia pulls internal stack data (per [[../product/features]] integration catalog). Bitsight observes externally.
- Onyxia's CPI Library and Framework Compliance Dashboard report on internal program performance. Bitsight's framework intelligence maps external exposure to frameworks.
- Bitsight's EASM and third-party visibility is outside Onyxia's scope. Honest.

Co-existence is the default pattern. A large-enterprise CISO commonly runs Bitsight for TPRM and an internal program-measurement layer separately.

Vault is silent on any closed-won deal where Onyxia displaced Bitsight. Not a natural displacement.

## Pricing signal

Custom quoted. Bitsight does not publicly disclose pricing. Typical enterprise deployments are six figures per year depending on vendor count and module scope. Forrester TEI study cited 297 percent ROI and 45 percent reduction in breach probability for Bitsight customers.

## Known customer base

3,500+ customers across 70+ countries. 38 percent of the Fortune 500. 4 of the top 5 investment banks. 180+ government agencies. Probable overlap with Onyxia's financial-services pipeline (First Horizon Bank, RGA Reinsurance, OCC) but no vault documentation confirms any specific deal.

## Onyxia's angle when this competitor is in the deal

Do not displace. Position as complementary.

Script per [[../methodology/objections]] objection #1: "Bitsight gives you the outside-in view of your organization and your vendors. Onyxia gives you the inside-out view of your own security program performance. Most of our large-enterprise customers run something like Bitsight alongside us. Not a replacement conversation."

If the only mandate is TPRM / vendor scoring, disqualify. Onyxia positioning does not address this dimension explicitly.
