# Vanta

## What they sell

Compliance automation platform. Automates evidence collection across 35+ frameworks including SOC 2, ISO 27001, HITRUST, GDPR, HIPAA, PCI. Core motion is continuous monitoring of cloud infrastructure and SaaS controls. Four pricing tiers: Essentials, Plus, Professional, Enterprise. Direct competitor to Drata in the SaaS compliance-automation category. Vanta reports customer ROI of 526 percent over three years and 129 percent compliance team productivity gain.

## Where they win (vs Onyxia)

- **Startups and SaaS through mid-market.** Vanta is the reflex buy for startups pursuing SOC 2. Deep go-to-market in the 10-500 FTE segment.
- **Multi-framework compliance automation.** 35+ frameworks supported, making it a durable buy as customers expand past SOC 2.
- **Trust-center and customer-facing transparency.** Vanta's Trust Center is a sales-enablement asset their customers actively use.

Buyer profile: security or engineering leader at a SaaS company, same pattern as Drata.

## Where Onyxia wins (vs them)

Different stage of customer maturity. Vanta is the gate to compliance. Onyxia is the CISO's program view once the company is past startup compliance mode.

Honest differentiation:
- Onyxia sells to Fortune 500 and leading-enterprise CISOs per [[../product/positioning]]. Vanta's installed base is overwhelmingly pre-enterprise.
- Onyxia's CPI Library (per [[../product/features]]) measures operational KPIs across the security stack: Detection & Response, Vulnerability Management, Training & Awareness, Cloud Security, IAM, Device Management. Vanta is anchored to audit-control evidence.
- Onyxia's board-reporting automation and OnyxAI predictive engine are not part of Vanta's positioning.

If a prospect has Vanta and is a Series C SaaS, they are probably not the Onyxia buyer yet. If they are a Fortune 500 subsidiary running Vanta for a single SOC 2 subsidiary audit, the parent CISO can still be a valid Onyxia buyer. Qualify on size and buyer title.

Vault is silent on any closed-won deal where Onyxia displaced Vanta.

## Pricing signal

Vanta lands in the $10K-$80K/year range as of March 2026. Median contract $20K/year based on 320 verified purchases on Vendr. Four plans: Essentials (startup), Plus, Professional, Enterprise. Add-on modules (vendor risk, questionnaire automation) increase spend. Partner channel can negotiate 20-40 percent off.

## Known customer base

Tens of thousands of customers, heavily weighted to SaaS mid-market. No logo overlap documented with Onyxia's closed-won corpus.

## Onyxia's angle when this competitor is in the deal

Same playbook as Drata. Different stage of the market. Don't try to displace.

Script: "Vanta handles your compliance automation well. Our buyer is the CISO managing a full security stack across the company, not the compliance function getting through an audit. If the program has moved past that, we're worth 15 minutes."

Disqualify any prospect still pre-first-audit or in early SaaS compliance mode. Revisit when they are 500+ FTE with a dedicated CISO and multiple frameworks live.
