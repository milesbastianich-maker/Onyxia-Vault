# ProcessUnity

## What they sell

Cloud-based third-party risk management and broader GRC suite. Modules: Third-Party Risk Management, Cybersecurity Program Management, Policy and Procedure Management, Enterprise Risk Management, Regulatory Compliance Management. Built around vendor-questionnaire workflow and the Global Risk Exchange (18,000+ vendors pre-assessed). Named a Leader in The Forrester Wave: Third-Party Risk Management Platforms, Q1 2026.

## Where they win (vs Onyxia)

- **Deep TPRM workflow.** Vendor questionnaire automation, assessment scoring, remediation tracking. This is their core.
- **Regulated industries buying TPRM-first.** Financial services and healthcare buyers whose primary driver is vendor risk reporting for regulators.
- **Large pre-assessed vendor database.** Global Risk Exchange gives them a network-effect moat on inbound vendor assessments.

Buyer profile: a TPRM or Vendor Risk Management function that owns a budget line for third-party assessments.

## Where Onyxia wins (vs them)

Onyxia is not a TPRM tool. The honest answer is: if the prospect's primary mandate is vendor assessments, ProcessUnity is a better fit for that specific job.

Where the two overlap is **board reporting on program posture**. Onyxia's board-ready reporting pulls from the full security stack, not just vendor data. Per [[../product/positioning]]: "We sit above your existing security stack, normalize the data, and auto-generate board-ready reporting."

Honest differentiation:
- Onyxia covers the internal program (SIEM, EDR, IAM, training, cloud) via [[../product/features]] CPI Library. ProcessUnity does not.
- Onyxia is positioned as the layer above GRC, not a GRC workflow replacement. Customers can run both.

Vault is silent on any closed-won deal where Onyxia replaced or displaced ProcessUnity specifically.

## Pricing signal

Subscription model, priced on user seats and vendor count. Not publicly listed. Enterprise deployments typically mid-six figures per year based on Gartner Peer Insights patterns.

## Known customer base

"Hundreds of organizations worldwide." Strong footprint in financial services and healthcare. Global Risk Exchange claims 18,000+ vendor participants. No specific logo overlap documented in Onyxia's closed-won corpus.

## Onyxia's angle when this competitor is in the deal

Do not position as a TPRM replacement. Position as the layer above TPRM that pulls ProcessUnity data into a unified board view alongside internal stack metrics.

Script: "ProcessUnity runs your vendor assessments well. The question is how you report on the full program, internal plus vendor, to the board in one view. That's where we come in alongside, not replace."

If the prospect's only concern is vendor risk, disqualify per [[../methodology/objections]] objection 7 pattern. Not our job.
