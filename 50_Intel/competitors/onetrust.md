# OneTrust

## What they sell

Privacy-first GRC and trust platform that expanded into TPRM, tech risk, ethics, and ESG. Modules include Tech Risk & Compliance, Third-Party Management, Privacy Automation, and Data Discovery. Named a Leader in the 2026 Gartner Magic Quadrant for Third-Party Risk Management Tools for Assurance Leaders. 14,000+ customers across 180 countries. Privacy module is the anchor; other modules attach to it.

## Where they win (vs Onyxia)

- **Privacy-regulated mid-market and enterprise.** Buyers whose primary driver is GDPR, CCPA, or data processing compliance. OneTrust was built around this.
- **Consent management.** Market-leading position there.
- **Multi-module land-and-expand.** Customers who already run OneTrust Privacy tend to add Tech Risk, TPRM, and ESG modules rather than buy a second platform.

Buyer profile: a Privacy Officer, DPO, or Chief Compliance Officer with a privacy-led mandate.

## Where Onyxia wins (vs them)

OneTrust's architectural center of gravity is privacy workflow. Reviews on Gartner Peer Insights note that for organizations whose primary driver is ERM or multi-framework security-program reporting rather than privacy, the platform's privacy roots are visible in the architecture.

Honest differentiation:
- Onyxia is a security-program measurement layer, not a privacy workflow tool. Different buyer, different mandate.
- Onyxia's CPI library (per [[../product/features]]) aligns to security frameworks: NIST CSF 2.0, HITRUST, SOC 2, PCI, HIPAA, FedRAMP. OneTrust's core strength is data / privacy frameworks.

Vault is silent on any closed-won deal where Onyxia replaced or displaced OneTrust specifically. [[../methodology/objections]] lists OneTrust as a named example in objection #7 ("we already bought a competitor"), meaning Miles has heard it in the field. Outcome of those deals is not captured in the vault.

## Pricing signal

Subscription with per-module pricing. Public data points: starts near $50K/year for a single module, scales to $250K+/year for multi-module enterprise deployments. Minimum $10K/year as of Q2 2026.

## Known customer base

14,000+ across 180 countries. Heavy presence in EU-regulated and consumer-data-heavy verticals. No specific logo overlap documented with Onyxia's closed-won corpus.

## Onyxia's angle when this competitor is in the deal

Do not try to displace OneTrust Privacy. Position on the security-program side.

Script per [[../methodology/objections]] objection #7 calibration: "How is OneTrust doing on automated security-program reporting to the board? Most of our customers keep OneTrust for privacy and run Onyxia alongside for the CISO board view."

If the prospect's security program reports through the DPO / Chief Privacy Officer, disqualify faster. Wrong buyer for Onyxia.
