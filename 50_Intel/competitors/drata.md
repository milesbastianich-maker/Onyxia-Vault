# Drata

## What they sell

Compliance automation platform for SOC 2, ISO 27001, HIPAA, PCI DSS, and GDPR. Continuous control monitoring collects evidence from cloud and SaaS integrations automatically. Positioning since 2025 has shifted toward an "agentic trust management platform" covering controls, policies, vendor risk, and customer-facing trust centers. Primarily sold to startups and mid-market SaaS companies pursuing their first or second framework certification.

## Where they win (vs Onyxia)

- **SaaS companies needing a first SOC 2 or ISO 27001.** Drata is the default buy for a Series A-C SaaS company with a 50-200 headcount getting audit-ready.
- **Audit-evidence automation.** Continuous control monitoring with auditor handoff is their core workflow.
- **Fast time to value for single-framework readiness.** Customers land SOC 2 Type II in months, not quarters.

Buyer profile: Head of Security, Head of Compliance, or Head of Engineering at a SaaS company approaching an audit deadline.

## Where Onyxia wins (vs them)

Different buyer, different job. Drata automates passing an audit. Onyxia measures the overall security program continuously for a CISO who is past the startup audit phase.

Honest differentiation:
- Onyxia's ICP is Fortune 500 and leading enterprise CISOs (per [[../product/positioning]]). Drata's ICP is growth-stage SaaS.
- Onyxia's CPI Library (per [[../product/features]]) measures program performance across domains like Detection & Response, Vulnerability Management, Identity & Access Management, Cloud Security, Device Management, Training & Awareness. Drata's automation is anchored to audit control evidence rather than CISO operational KPIs.
- Onyxia supports NIST CSF 2.0, HITRUST, SOC 2, PCI, HIPAA, FedRAMP with a one-click framework switch. Drata is deeper on audit-evidence workflow for SOC 2 and ISO 27001 specifically.

If a prospect is actively preparing for a first SOC 2, Drata is probably the right tool. Disqualify Onyxia against that buyer and re-approach when they are post-audit and the CISO needs a program view.

Vault is silent on any closed-won deal where Onyxia displaced Drata.

## Pricing signal

Foundation plan starts near $15K/year for up to 50 FTEs with one pre-mapped framework. Single-framework pricing for companies 10-85 FTEs typically $7,500-$9,000/year. Mid-market (50-200 FTEs) pursuing SOC 2 Type II commonly lands $20K-$45K/year. Auditor fees are separate, $8K-$25K for SOC 2 Type II depending on size.

## Known customer base

Thousands of growth-stage SaaS customers. Not an enterprise incumbent. No logo overlap documented with Onyxia's closed-won corpus.

## Onyxia's angle when this competitor is in the deal

If the prospect says "we use Drata," Onyxia positioning does not address this head-on. The honest angle: Onyxia is a different stage of maturity than Drata. Drata gets you to the audit. Onyxia runs after you are past the audit and you need a program view.

Script: "Drata is great for getting you through SOC 2. Onyxia is for when the CISO needs the ongoing program view across the whole stack, not just audit evidence. Usually a later-stage buy."

If the prospect is early-stage and still audit-focused, disqualify per [[../playbook/closed-lost-patterns]] time-frame pattern. Re-approach in 12-18 months.
