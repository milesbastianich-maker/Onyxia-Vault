## Healthcare

### 1. Medtronic breach confirmed — ShinyHunters, 9M+ records, SEC 8-K filed April 24

ShinyHunters claimed theft of 9M+ records from Medtronic corporate IT on April 18. Medtronic confirmed unauthorized access April 24 and filed a Form 8-K. ### 4. OCR enforcement scope expanded May 1 — risk management now a formal enforcement target

Per OCR Senior Advisor for Cybersecurity guidance effective May 1, 2026, OCR formal enforcement now targets risk management (acting on risks) with the same civil monetary penalty exposure as risk analysis (knowing risks). Previously, programs could document risks without demonstrable remediation. That gap is closed.

Any covered entity that can show an assessed risk but cannot show a remediation action or accepted residual risk is now CMP-exposed.

## Critical Infrastructure

### 1. TSA Pipeline SD-2021-02F expired May 2 — operators must verify compliance with 01G

TSA Security Directive Pipeline-2021-02F expired May 2, 2026 as scheduled. It was superseded by Pipeline-2021-01G, issued January 9, 2026, effective January 16, 2026 through January 15, 2027. The 01G covers the same core obligations: cybersecurity coordinator, CISA incident reporting, vulnerability assessment, Cybersecurity Implementation Plan.

Pipeline operators running compliance programs against 02F must verify they have transitioned to 01G. Failure to reference the current directive creates a documentation gap in any CISA audit or TSA assessment.

Source: [TSA SD Pipeline-2021-01G](https://www.tsa.gov/sites/default/files/signed_security-directive-pipeline-2021-01g-and-transmittal-memo_508c.pdf) | [[../regulatory-calendar]]

### 2. CIRCIA final rule — delay past May 2026 now likely, CISA at 38% staffing

CISA canceled CIRCIA town halls in March-April due to DHS appropriations lapse. The agency is operating at approximately 38% staffing per Cybersecurity Dive. The May 2026 final rule target is now at risk of further slippage. Core obligations are expected to remain unchanged: 72-hour incident reporting from "reasonable belief" a covered incident occurred, 24-hour ransomware payment reporting. Applies to ~300,000+ entities across all 16 critical infrastructure sectors.

The delay removes the near-term compliance deadline but does not remove the direction. Organizations without a documented incident response and reporting workflow are building technical debt against a near-certain mandate.

Sources: [CyberScoop — CIRCIA delay](https://cyberscoop.com/cisa-pushes-final-cyber-incident-reporting-rule-to-may-2026/) | [Cybersecurity Dive — CISA 2026 challenges](https://www.cybersecuritydive.com/news/cisa-7-biggest-challenges-2026/809088/)

### 3. Iranian APT — water, energy, government PLC attacks ongoing (CISA aa26-097a)

Since at least March 2026, an Iranian-affiliated APT group has been disrupting programmable logic controllers across water and wastewater, energy, and government facilities. CISA advisory aa26-097a documents attacks on Rockwell Automation and Allen-Bradley PLCs. Victims experienced data manipulation, operational downtime, and financial loss. CISA direction: remove PLCs from direct internet exposure, use secure gateways and firewalls.

For any OT-connected utility, pipeline, or transit operator: internet-exposed PLCs are the documented initial access vector. This is not a theoretical risk. CISA has identified active victims.

Source: [CISA advisory aa26-097a](https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/cybersecurity-advisories/aa26-097a)

### 4. UAE coordinated attack wave — Iran-aligned, 600K+ daily attacks on ports, courts, financial platforms

An Iran-aligned coordinated attack wave hit UAE and Gulf region infrastructure in April 2026. 600,000+ daily attacks targeted government systems, ports, courts, and financial platforms. Transaction delays and access interruptions reported. This demonstrates nation-state-grade attack volume now hitting critical infrastructure at scale across multiple sectors simultaneously.

Relevant context for any US critical infrastructure prospect: geopolitical escalation is driving sustained operational attacks, not just espionage.
