## Healthcare

### 1. Brockton Hospital ransomware attack — Anubis group, 2 TB exfil

Signature Healthcare's Brockton Hospital (MA) was hit April 6. ED went on divert. Chemotherapy cancelled April 7. Anubis ransomware group claimed responsibility April 9 and asserts 2 TB of patient data exfiltrated. Hospital remained on downtime procedures for at least two weeks.

This is a clean peer-breach trigger for all active healthcare accounts. The pattern: ambulance diversion + treatment cancellation produces board-level urgency in the days following the incident.

### 2. HIPAA Security Rule final rule — OCR still targeting May 2026

OCR has kept May 2026 on its regulatory agenda despite industry pushback. If finalized in May, the rule becomes effective approximately July–August 2026. Most compliance deadlines fall within 180 days of effective date (end of 2026 or early 2027).

Key requirements: mandatory asset inventory + network map (updated every 12 months), encryption at rest and in transit, MFA, annual pen test, 72-hour restoration objective, 24-hour access-change notifications, annual compliance audit.

This is the single biggest structural compliance forcing function in the vertical right now. Any covered entity that cannot show an instrumented asset inventory is already behind.

Active accounts affected: [[../../10_Accounts/FMOL Health]], [[../../10_Accounts/People Inc]], [[../../10_Accounts/Homestead Smart]], [[../../10_Accounts/GenomOncology]].

Source: [HIPAA Journal — HIPAA updates 2026](https://www.hipaajournal.com/hipaa-updates-hipaa-changes/) | [Clearwater Security — enforcement 2026](https://clearwatersecurity.com/blog/hipaa-security-rule-enforcement-2026/)

### 3. Stryker medtech attack — Iran-linked, Active Directory destroyed

Stryker reported worldwide disruption to its Microsoft environment on March 11. Iran-aligned Handala group accessed via Microsoft Intune, destroyed Active Directory. Not ransomware. A pure disruption attack. Manufacturing and shipping were offline. Stryker declared itself "fully operational" in April. CISA issued guidance on endpoint management system vulnerabilities in response.

Relevant for healthcare organizations that run hybrid IT estates with third-party medical device vendors. Vendor AD compromise = downstream covered entity exposure.

Source: [SecurityWeek — Iran-linked Stryker attack](https://www.securityweek.com/iran-linked-hacker-attack-on-stryker-disrupted-manufacturing-and-shipping/) | [CISA via Industrial Cyber](https://industrialcyber.co/cisa/cisa-flags-rising-threats-to-endpoint-management-systems-after-stryker-breach-urges-stronger-defense/)

## Financial Services

### 1. LPL Financial breach disclosed — April 24, 2026

LPL Financial disclosed a breach that occurred November 10, 2025 and was discovered 10 days later. Phishing-distributed malware on advisor devices. Unauthorized third-party access to the advisor portal. 1,581 clients affected. Unauthorized securities transactions and financial transfers occurred. LPL stopped activity, restored impacted account positions, and implemented new technical safeguards.

This is a direct peer-breach trigger for wealth management and registered investment adviser firms. The attack path — phishing to device to portal — is not novel, but the unauthorized transaction layer makes it material under SEC and FINRA standards.

### 2. NYDFS Part 500 April 15 certification window closed

April 15, 2026 was the first year in which covered entities certified compliance with ALL Part 500 amendments, including the MFA and asset inventory requirements that took effect November 1, 2025. Entities that acknowledged non-compliance are now subject to $2,500/day penalties under NY Banking Law. CISO board-reporting requirement is now in effect annually.

The post-deadline window (now through ~June) is historically when enforcement risk converts to procurement urgency. Prospects who missed gap items on the April 15 form are actively remediating.

## Critical Infrastructure

### 1. CISA alert: Iranian hackers attacking water + energy PLCs — April 7, 2026

CISA issued urgent guidance after Iranian-linked actors attacked water and energy infrastructure using internet-exposed programmable logic controllers. Specifically Rockwell Automation and Allen-Bradley PLCs. Attacks caused data manipulation, operational downtime, and financial loss. CISA's direction: remove PLCs from direct internet exposure immediately, use secure gateways and firewalls.

This is the most actionable new threat advisory in the sector this week. Any OT-connected utility or pipeline operator with internet-facing PLCs is exposed.

Source: [Tom's Hardware / CISA PLC alert](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/cyber-security/us-cybersecurity-agency-issues-an-urgent-alert-as-iranian-hackers-attack-critical-infrastructure-cisa-guidance-warns-organizations-to-immediately-shield-certain-programmable-logic-controllers-from-the-internet-to-thwart-future-attacks)

### 2. Itron SEC 8-K filed April 13, 2026 — unauthorized access to internal network

Itron (ITRI, utility smart metering + grid management tech) filed a material cybersecurity incident disclosure under Item 1.05 of Form 8-K. An unauthorized third party accessed internal systems on approximately April 13. Itron activated its response plan, notified law enforcement, and remediated. Customer-hosted systems unaffected. Operations continued. Insurance expected to cover the majority of direct costs.

Itron's product base serves electric, gas, and water utilities globally. Its disclosure is a proof point that critical infrastructure technology vendors are now a priority target. Supply chain visibility matters for any operator that relies on Itron infrastructure.

Source: [BleepingComputer — Itron breach](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/american-utility-firm-itron-discloses-breach-of-internal-it-network/) | [SEC 8-K filing](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/780571/000119312526175249/d125229d8k.htm)

### 3. CIRCIA final rule likely delayed past May 2026

CISA canceled all CIRCIA town hall meetings scheduled between March 9 and April 2 due to a DHS appropriations lapse. The original May 2026 target for the final rule is now at risk of further slippage. Core reporting obligations (72-hour incident report, 24-hour ransomware payment report) are expected to remain unchanged in the final rule. All 16 critical infrastructure sectors will be covered.

The delay means no new compliance obligation lands this quarter, but the direction is fixed. Organizations without a documented incident response and reporting workflow are building technical debt against a near-certain mandate.

Source: [Federal News Network — CIRCIA town halls](https://federalnewsnetwork.com/cybersecurity/2026/03/cisa-delays-cyber-incident-reporting-town-halls-due-to-shutdown/) | [CyberScoop — CIRCIA delay](https://cyberscoop.com/cisa-pushes-final-cyber-incident-reporting-rule-to-may-2026/)

### 4. TSA Pipeline SD-2021-02F expires May 2, 2026

The TSA Security Directive covering pipeline cybersecurity expires in 6 days. Renewal or replacement is expected. The TSA NPRM permanent surface transportation rule (proposed November 2024) has not been finalized. Pipeline operators face a brief regulatory gap window between the expiring directive and any replacement instrument.
