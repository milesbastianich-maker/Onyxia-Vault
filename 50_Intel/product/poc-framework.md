# POC Framework

Derived from the Premera POC criteria document. This is the template for how Onyxia runs proof-of-concepts.

## Duration
**2-4 weeks.** Not longer. Scoping creep is the POC killer.

## What integrates in the POC window

Customer selects which technologies to connect from the 9 security domains. Typical POC is 5-10 integrations to prove the data-fabric thesis.

See [[features]] for the full integration catalog.

## Capabilities tested in every POC

- **Frameworks** — pick one or more: NIST, FedRamp, HIPAA, SOC 2, PCI, or Custom (ISO)
- **Security Stack Map** — demonstrate coverage management + CPI-level performance per cell
- **Budget View** — demonstrate spend visibility + ROI measurement
- **Benchmarking** — on-demand using historical + real-time, compared to industry SLAs
- **OnyxAI** — predictive insights, strategic improvement suggestions, risk reduction

## POC goals (standard)

1. Integrate X different technologies (X = customer-agreed number)
2. Populate Security Stack Map and Budget View
3. Generate a report
4. Adjust SLA based on customer policy

## POC outcomes (what we deliver)

1. Identify areas of over-investment or inefficiency
2. Identify gaps for additional investment
3. Identify KPIs for prioritization

These are the three outputs to close on. Every POC review meeting should restate these.

## How to run the POC well (operator notes)

- **Scope integrations up-front.** Get the list signed off before kickoff. Changes during POC = delays.
- **Set the success criteria before integration.** Customer commits to what "success" looks like. Otherwise you demo forever without a close.
- **Weekly checkpoint.** Status meeting every 7 days with Jigar-equivalent and one exec observer. Keeps urgency up.
- **Deliver the benchmarking story in week 2.** "Here's how you compare to similar orgs" is the wow moment. Reserve it.
- **End-of-POC exec readout.** 30 minutes. CISO + CFO/GC. Walk through the three outcomes. Close for next-quarter start.

## POC anti-patterns (lose-risk)

- POC drifts past 4 weeks without exec escalation
- Customer can't name the Economic Buyer (check MEDDPICC before POC kickoff)
- Integrations team is unavailable during the window
- No weekly checkpoint cadence = deal goes dark

## When POC is the wrong ask

- Prospect has < 4 weeks until a board meeting and needs something ready (offer a guided demo + sandbox instead)
- Prospect won't commit 1-2 engineering hours per week to the POC
- No executive sponsor on their side

In those cases, close the meeting with a demo + champion call, not a POC.
