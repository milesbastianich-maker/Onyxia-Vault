# Customer Quotes (Verbatim)

Always use these over paraphrase. Quoted customer language has weight that our language does not.

## The 160-hours quote (healthcare)

> "Onyxia puts us in a much better position to assess our security program and exposures. With Onyxia, we are able to save at least 160 working hours a month that one full-time analyst would traditionally spend on manual reporting and instead, focus more time on our actual risk management strategy."
>
> — **CISO of a leading not-for-profit healthcare client** (13,000+ employees, 4 US regions, top-ranked hospitals, primary + outpatient + cardiology + cancer + orthopedics + women's health)

Use for: any healthcare prospect, any prospect asking about ROI of reporting automation.

## The 40-hours-per-week quote (insurtech)

> "Before Onyxia, our team spent at least 40 hours per week on manual reporting and data aggregation. Onyxia has significantly reduced this burden, freeing up valuable time for our strategic initiatives. Onyxia took us from the 'spreadsheet from hell' to automation to now, we can actually track and predict where things are going to go. When I started having conversations with other business units, and even executives, they said: 'This is amazing.'"
>
> — **CISO of Leading Insurtech Company** (likely CCC Intelligent Solutions, confirmed closed-won at $120K)

Use for: any fintech, insurtech, mid-market SaaS. "Spreadsheet from hell" is a phrase that lands.

## The "intuitive" quote (Hashal Mehta)

> "With Onyxia, everything is intuitive; the team now spends time analyzing results rather than populating them. Onyxia helps us understand where we're excelling and where we need more focus. We can reprioritize efforts based on real data rather than assumptions."
>
> — **Hashal Mehta, VP & CISO** (employer not specified in doc)

Use for: when the objection is "we already have tools, we don't need another dashboard."

## The immediate-value quote

> "Onyxia's platform has brought us immediate value. The deployment was straightforward, the total cost of ownership is minimal, and we now have a complete cyber dashboard that we can count on to provide accurate and meaningful insights into where our cybersecurity program stands."
>
> — Customer (unattributed in whitepaper)

Use for: objections about deployment complexity, TCO, time-to-value.

## The "spreadsheet from hell → automation" shorthand

When Miles is pitching and someone is nodding about their manual reporting burden, drop this phrase: "Our customers call it the spreadsheet-from-hell to automation shift." It's a specific customer phrase, not a marketing phrase.
