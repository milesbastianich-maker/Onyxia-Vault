# Power Phrases

Concrete, vivid, peer-toned language with a point of view. Phrases that pass the "would Miles actually send this" smell test. Grounded in verbatim winning openers (Yosef SEC checklist, Sivan Q2 reporting) and the customer-verbatim quotes in `50_Intel/product/quotes.md`.

## Openers that earn attention

**Regulation-timed:**
- "The SEC rule dropped December 18th. Most of the public filers I talk to still do not have an incident-disclosure playbook."
- "HIPAA Security Rule finalization target is May 2026. Asset inventory, network map, 72-hour restoration. Most programs are not instrumented for any of the three."
- "CMMC enforcement hits November 10. Half the DIB supply chain is still treating it like a 2027 problem."
- "DORA has been in force since January. UK fin-svcs friends are still chasing their vendor inventory."

**Forcing function:**
- "Board meeting next Tuesday. What's the one slide that would make the CFO sign the POC?"
- "Q2 closes Friday. Most CISOs are still PowerPointing their program metrics by hand."
- "New CISO 30 days in. The strategy slide is usually the hardest one in the first 90."

**Peer framing:**
- "As a former CISO, Sivan built this because…" (verbatim from Sivan's winning CWT opener)
- "One of our customers runs a 15-hospital system and told us…"
- "Another CISO in your sector described it to us as…" (always ground in `quotes.md`)

## Specific beats generic

Bad → Better:

- "your digital transformation" → "your Epic rollout"
- "your security posture" → "your CyberArk + Splunk deployment"
- "your compliance program" → "your HIPAA §164.308 risk-analysis evidence"
- "your analyst team" → "your 12-person SOC"
- "recent industry trends" → "the Warby Parker OCR settlement"
- "board reporting" → "the slide you show your audit committee"
- "your threat landscape" → "the 30% third-party involvement in this year's DBIR"

## Value-first language (no-ask hooks)

From the Yosef SEC Checklist opener (2023-12-21, deal closed $100K):

- "I wanted to share a resource with you with zero upfront ask."
- "Originally created for our public enterprise customers."
- "Don't like clicking links? I'd be happy to show it to you when timely and relevant."

This is a template for any lead-magnet-style outbound. The "zero upfront ask" phrasing is verbatim and lifts cleanly.

## Peer-question phrases

The questions a CISO would ask another CISO. Not vendor questions.

- "Who's pushing the board slide each quarter?"
- "What does your OCR Phase 3 answer look like?"
- "If an 8-K hits your desk tomorrow, who owns the first 24 hours?"
- "Who has the spreadsheet right now?" (framework-mapping hook)
- "What did the board ask at the last read-out?"

## CTAs that convert (grounded in `cta-ladder.md`)

**Passive (low signal):**
- "Curious if this is on your radar."
- "Happy to share if useful."

**Specific (default):**
- "Worth 15 minutes next Tuesday or Thursday?"
- "Can you join me for a brief 30-minute call to explore…" (verbatim from Sivan's CWT opener)

**Peer-call (cap: 2-3/week, saturate carefully):**
- "Sivan would be happy to spend 20 minutes CISO-to-CISO. No slides, no demo."

## Close-the-loop language (post-meeting follow-up)

From the Brian Fisher CCC / WellStar template pattern:

- "It was great meeting you and thank you for taking the time to speak."
- "Below I've attached our current CPI's and integrations so you can see what can be deployed right out of the box."
- "On our next call we are happy to dive into how our [specific capability] will work."
- "In the meantime if you have any additional questions please feel free to reach out."

## Words that punch (swap-outs)

- help → get to
- assist → handle
- utilize → use
- commence → start
- terminate → end
- facilitate → make easier, or delete
- implement → roll out, ship, deploy
- endeavor → try
- subsequently → then
- in order to → to
- due to the fact that → because
- in the event that → if
- at this point in time → now
- prior to → before
- going forward → from here

## Buyer-voice mirroring (2026 CISO LinkedIn phrasing to mirror, not overuse)

From contemporary CISO public posts (late 2025 / early 2026 scan). Use sparingly, do not parrot.

- "I spend half my week proving we are not negligent."
- "Board doesn't want a dashboard. Board wants a sentence."
- "My program is only as good as our worst vendor's program."
- "The audit is the easy part. The audit prep is the six-month job."
- "I am not buying more tools. I am buying fewer tools that do more."

The last one is particularly load-bearing for Onyxia's positioning — we're a layer, not another tool.
