## Regulatory surface

- **SEC Cybersecurity Disclosure Rule (active).** All SEC registrants — including publicly traded REITs — must file Form 8-K within 4 business days of a material incident. Annual Form 10-K must include cyber risk management program, board oversight, and management expertise disclosures (Item 106, Reg S-K). FY 2026 SEC exam priorities flag disclosure quality reviews. Source: [SEC.gov Rule S7-09-22](https://www.sec.gov/rules-regulations/2023/07/s7-09-22).
- **Board-disclosure cascade.** Even private CRE operators face LP/investor-driven cyber disclosure questions modeled on the public-company standard. REIT boards are asking the same governance questions the SEC has codified.
- **Churn signal — Brookfield Properties.** In HubSpot "Churned" bucket. Do a why-did-they-leave review before prospecting adjacent accounts. Source: [[../hunt-research/2026-04-22-closed-won-nonhealthcare-synthesis]].

## What real estate CISOs/CIOs care about

1. **Board-ready annual cyber narrative.** REIT boards and audit committees now ask for the same 10-K-grade cyber governance narrative that the SEC mandates in writing. Onyxia builds it.
2. **Multi-property + multi-tenant risk visibility.** Large CRE operators (Simon Property, Brookfield, Sun Communities) have dozens of building management systems, tenant networks, and third-party vendors per property. Single-dashboard view matters.
3. **SEC incident materiality workflow.** The 4-business-day 8-K clock starts when the CISO determines materiality. That determination needs a documented process. Onyxia's continuous monitoring feeds the materiality analysis.
4. **Investor/LP reporting.** Non-public operators answer to private equity sponsors who now ask SEC-grade cyber questions even without a filing obligation.

## Who wins the meeting

Primary: CISO, VP IT / CIO (smaller operators may not have a CISO).
Secondary thread: GC, CFO, Audit Committee liaison.
Both Gaia and Silverstein were closed by Sivan personally — peer-intro driven. Cold outreach alone is a long road. Best angle: warm intro via Sivan → CRE CISO or GC → board-framing conversation.

## Competitive context

Incumbents in real estate IT skew toward property management and building automation vendors (Yardi, MRI Software, JLL Technologies). GRC tools are typically absent or Excel-based. Cyber tooling tends to be ad hoc. Onyxia walks into greenfield board-reporting territory.
