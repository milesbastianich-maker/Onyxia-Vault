## Regulatory surface

- **GLBA Safeguards Rule (FTC, active since June 6, 2023).** All Title IV institutions of higher education must maintain a written information security program (WISP), conduct periodic risk assessments, enforce access controls, test safeguards, and oversee third-party service providers. DoE enforces via the SAIG Agreement and Federal Single Audit. Non-compliance can trigger loss of Title IV funding. Source: [Student Privacy Compass GLBA Guide](https://studentprivacycompass.org/higher-education-compliance-with-updates-to-the-glba-safeguards-rule/).
- **NIST 800-171 Rev 3 (DoD research contracts, late 2026–early 2027).** Universities handling Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) from DoD research grants are bound by federal regulation to implement NIST 800-171. DoD April 2025 memo assigned ODP values for Rev 3. Implementation as contractor minimum requirement expected late 2026–early 2027. Research universities that fall behind face loss of DoD contracts. Source: [UpGuard NIST 800-171 Rev 3](https://www.upguard.com/blog/nist-800-171-rev3), [Holland & Knight DoD ODP memo](https://www.hklaw.com/en/insights/publications/2025/05/dod-publishes-organization-defined-parameters-for-nist-sp).
- **FERPA.** Student record privacy. FTC ruling: FERPA compliance satisfies GLBA's privacy requirement but NOT its security requirement. Both run independently.
- **State-level tightening.** NY, CA, TX breach notification requirements apply to universities as data controllers for student + employee records.

## What higher education CISOs / CIOs care about

1. **Multi-framework consolidation.** Running GLBA + NIST 800-171 + FERPA + state law simultaneously with a lean IT team. This is Onyxia's multi-framework board-reporting pitch.
2. **Board of Trustees reporting.** University governance = Board of Trustees or Regents. They ask the same board-governance questions that corporate boards ask post-SEC rule.
3. **DoD research contract protection.** Research universities cannot afford to lose DoD grants. NIST 800-171 Rev 3 is an existential compliance event for R1 research institutions.
4. **Third-party vendor risk.** Universities use hundreds of SaaS vendors across departments. GLBA requires third-party oversight. Onyxia's vendor-risk layer applies.

## Competitive context

Higher education IT security tends to run on:
- Industry-standard GRC tools (ServiceNow IRM, Archer) at large R1 universities
- EDUCAUSE-recommended frameworks (NIST CSF, CIS Controls) for smaller schools
- Excel + SharePoint for compliance tracking at mid-tier schools

Onyxia enters at the reporting layer above the technical tools — same positioning as healthcare. The EDUCAUSE community is the access channel: presenting at EDUCAUSE or sourcing intros from their CISO working group is the path.
