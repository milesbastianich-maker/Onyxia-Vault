# LinkedIn Outreach Playbook

Canonical rules for every LinkedIn touch Miles or the drafter sends. Connect notes, DMs, comments, profile updates. The drafter MUST consult this file before writing any LinkedIn message.

## Connect requests

1. **Blank requests beat generic notes.** Requests without a note get 5% higher acceptance than templated ones. Add a note ONLY when it is genuinely personalized.

2. **Do not start with their name.** Everyone does it. Open with an observation about something specific to them.

3. **Send within 24 hours of a profile view.** That window gets 3x higher acceptance.

4. **"Saw your post about X" beats company or role mentions.** 2x performance.

5. **Job changers in last 90 days accept 40% more.** New seat, new budget, new problems.

6. **Multi-thread first, decision-maker second.** Connect with 2-3 people at a company before the EB. Instant mutual connections do the work.

7. **Mutual-connection name-drop boosts response 45%.** Only if they actually know the person. Lying is worse than blank.

8. **Connect immediately after commenting on their post.** Feels natural, not salesy.

9. **Withdraw pending requests after 3 weeks.** Re-send fresh.

## Profile hygiene

10. **Headline is searchable.** "Helping B2B teams 3X reply rates" beats "Sales Director." Prospects search problems, not titles.

11. **First 40 characters of headline show on mobile requests.** Front-load value.

12. **Custom URL ranks higher in LinkedIn and Google search.**

13. **Orange and yellow backgrounds get 20%+ more clicks than blue or gray.**

14. **Video in Featured section increases dwell time.** LinkedIn reads that as engagement.

## Messages (DMs and follow-ups)

15. **First 100 characters show in notifications.** If they read like a pitch, nobody opens.

16. **"Partnership" kills response by 30%.** Reads as "I want to sell you."

17. **"Pick your brain" is the most hated phrase on LinkedIn.** Never use it.

18. **"Quick call" reads as 30 minutes.** "15 minutes, I'll be brief" works better.

19. **One question at the end boosts response 50%.** Only one. Multiples overwhelm.

20. **Hand-off line that gets forwarded:** "If this isn't your area, happy to connect with whoever handles X."

21. **You/your should outnumber I/we in every message.** Count them.

## Signals to watch (warm prospects hiding in plain sight)

22. **Post commenters are 7x more likely to respond to DMs than cold prospects.**

23. **Someone engaging with a competitor's content is showing buying intent.** Reach out now.

24. **Second-degree connections engaging with your content want to connect.** Send the request.

25. **Someone following your company page but not you personally is researching.** Warm prospect.

26. **Job changes in your network = hot leads.** New budget, new problems. LinkedIn notifies on these.

## Cadence

27. **If they accept but do not reply, wait 5-7 days.** Same-day follow-ups feel aggressive.

28. **Connect with their commenters first.** Multiple mutual connections instantly.

29. **GIFs boost response 30%, but only after rapport.** Never cold.

30. **Tuesday through Thursday requests get 20% higher acceptance.** Weekends worst.

## Banned phrases (LinkedIn-specific, additive to the email banned list)

- "Pick your brain"
- "Partnership"
- "Quick call"
- "I'd love to connect" (generic)
- "Hope this finds you well"
- "Wanted to reach out" (any LinkedIn variant)
- "Loved your post" (generic without a specific reference to the post)

## Drafter rules for LinkedIn output

When the drafter generates a LinkedIn connect note or DM:

- **Connect notes:** default to BLANK. Only add a note if it can clear the personalization bar (specific observation about a post, mutual connection by name, recent job change, or profile-viewed-recently context).
- **If a note is added:** under 200 characters total, opens with an observation NOT a name, ends with one question or one hand-off line.
- **DMs:** under 400 characters for first DM. One question. You/your outnumber I/we.
- **Voice rules:** all v2 cold email bans still apply (no `+`, no abbreviations, no em dashes, no banned email phrases).
- **Output format:** return the connect note (or DM) as plain text, the character count, and a one-line note on which LinkedIn rule it leans on.

## When to choose LinkedIn over email

Channel-switch from email to LinkedIn when:
- Prospect has not replied to 3+ emails AND has any LinkedIn activity in the past 30 days
- Prospect is a job-changer in past 90 days (LinkedIn beats email for fresh seats)
- Cold first-touch where the prospect has commented on a relevant post recently (warm signal)
- Email address is unverified or bouncing

Stay on email when:
- Active deal with prior calendar acceptance
- Existing thread with reciprocal replies
- Event invite to a confirmed RSVP list (email is the formal channel)
