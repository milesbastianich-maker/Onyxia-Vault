# Onyxia Pain Library

This is the FIRST file the drafter reads on any cold-email task. Pick the spine pain for THIS contact at THIS company at THIS moment. Build the email around the pain. Regulations and frameworks get a one-sentence cameo only when they sharpen the timing pressure on the chosen spine.

The drafter must write three lines in its return payload BEFORE drafting any body text:
1. **Spine pain (named, from this library):**
2. **Why THIS pain for THIS person:** (one sentence, contact-specific)
3. **Onyxia's response in plain English:** (one sentence, no abstract nouns)

If the drafter cannot write all three clearly, it returns a research request to the operator. No draft.

---

## The 8 spine pains

Onyxia's actual coverage. Each pain is a real thing a real CISO complains about in real meetings. None of them are "you need compliance automation." The pain is what hurts; Onyxia is the relief.

### 1. Manual reporting is eating my analyst hours

**Plain English:** Your team spends days every month manually pulling numbers from 30 tools, building slides for the board, and answering audit questions. The work does not scale. Headcount cannot grow with the load.

**Who feels it most:**
- GRC directors at audit-heavy shops
- Mid-market CISOs without a dedicated reporting function
- CISOs at lean-team companies (high revenue, low headcount)

**Triggers that surface this pain:**
- Audit cycle approach (any framework, any quarter)
- Board prep season
- Headcount freeze announced
- Framework recertification kickoff
- Compliance hire posted (signals overload)

**Onyxia's response (1 sentence, drafter can use this verbatim or adapt):**
> Onyxia auto-generates the board report your team is building by hand right now.

**Proof (verbatim, must be quoted not paraphrased):**

> "With Onyxia, we are able to save at least 160 working hours a month that one full-time analyst would traditionally spend on manual reporting and instead, focus more time on our actual risk management strategy."
>
> — CISO of a leading not-for-profit healthcare client (13,000+ employees, 4 US regions)

> "Before Onyxia, our team spent at least 40 hours per week on manual reporting and data aggregation. Onyxia took us from the 'spreadsheet from hell' to automation."
>
> — CISO of a leading insurtech (CCC Intelligent Solutions, $120K closed-won)

**Verticals where this pain is sharpest:** Healthcare, insurtech, fintech, mid-market SaaS, any audit-heavy shop.

### 2. I have 30+ tools and no idea which ones are actually doing their job

**Plain English:** You have a security stack you inherited or grew over five years. Three tools overlap. Two might not even be live anymore. When the board asks "is this asset protected," you guess. When the CFO asks "are we getting our money's worth on the 12 vendors," you guess again.

**Who feels it most:**
- Mature CISOs who inherited a stack
- Post-M&A CISOs holding two stacks bolted together
- VPs of Security running ops day-to-day
- Anyone whose budget renewal is coming up

**Triggers:**
- M&A close (acquirer side)
- Budget renewal cycle
- Post-breach posture review
- New CISO inheritance
- Renewal date approaching on 3+ tools in the same quarter

**Onyxia's response:**
> Onyxia auto-renders your security stack map in 30 minutes, so you see overlap, gaps, and orphan tools without surveying your team.

**Proof:**
- Stack Map feature: charts every integrated tool into one of 30 cells (asset type × NIST 2.0 pillar). Auto-generated on integration.
- Asset Coverage Analyzer: measures and compares coverage across all devices.
- Hashal Mehta quote: "Everything is intuitive; the team now spends time analyzing results rather than populating them."

**Verticals where sharpest:** Manufacturing, financial services, healthcare hospital systems, any post-M&A enterprise.

### 3. When something happens, the disclosure clock starts and we are scrambling

**Plain English:** You get hacked. The SEC says you have 4 business days to tell the public. Your GC, your CFO, your CISO, and your board have to agree on whether the incident is material. Today that decision is being built from scratch under pressure, in a war room, while the news cycle ticks.

**Who feels it most:**
- Public-company CISOs (any market cap)
- General Counsels at public co
- CFOs at post-incident shops
- Board audit committee chairs
- Cyber insurance renewers

**Triggers:**
- SEC Item 1.05 filer status
- Post-incident any company in adjacent sub-vertical
- Cyber insurance renewal underway
- New board chair appointed
- Class-action filing in adjacent sub-vertical
- 8-K filed by competitor

**Onyxia's response:**
> Onyxia keeps the materiality evidence ready before the clock starts, so your GC, CFO, and CISO have one clean answer instead of four versions.

**Proof:**
- Yosef Adatto's CWT cold (Dec 2023, 3 days after SEC rule enacted) closed at $100K
- Board Reporting feature: one-click full report, monthly/quarterly preset
- Framework Compliance Dashboard: regulatory framework view layered on real data

**Verticals where sharpest:** Public-company finserv, public healthcare, public manufacturing, public life sciences, public defense primes, public retail.

### 4. I just took this seat and I do not actually know where we stand

**Plain English:** You took the CISO chair 90 days ago. Your boss is going to ask "where are we on [framework / threat / risk]" and right now you cannot give a clean answer. Your team is too busy to do a 6-month assessment. You cannot afford to look unprepared in front of the board, the CIO, the CFO. Inheriting a black-box program is the worst part of any new-CISO role.

**Who feels it most:**
- New CISOs (under 12 months in role)
- New VPs of Security
- New Directors of GRC (new-leader path)
- New board chairs (asking the CISO downstream)

**Triggers:**
- Position start date within last 180 days (LinkedIn, ZoomInfo)
- New CIO or CFO above them (new boss = new questions)
- Board chair turnover at their company
- Recent CISO hire announced publicly
- 90-day mark approaching after start date

**Onyxia's response:**
> Onyxia gives you a clean baseline of your program in 30 days, not the 9 months it would take your team.

**Proof:**
- Marlon Clarke / Cross Country canonical email pattern (new CIO transition)
- Leilani Farol / First Horizon canonical email pattern (GRC-to-CISO at $84B-asset bank)
- Sivan as former CISO peer-call: "20-minute peer call with someone navigating the same moment"

**Verticals where sharpest:** Anywhere a new CISO landed in the last 6 months. Cross-vertical.

### 5. Same evidence, different auditors, different formats, every quarter

**Plain English:** Your GRC team produces the same control evidence three or four times a year. Once for HIPAA. Once for ISO 27001. Once for SOC 2. Once for the cyber insurer. The actual security work does not change between them. The paperwork does. Your team rebuilds the spreadsheet from scratch each cycle.

**Who feels it most:**
- GRC directors at multi-framework shops
- Healthcare CISOs (HIPAA + state regs + payer audits)
- Higher-ed CISOs (HIPAA + GLBA + FERPA + research IP frameworks)
- Life sciences CISOs (FDA + SEC + 21 CFR + state)
- Federal contractors (CMMC + NIST 800-171 + ITAR)

**Triggers:**
- Framework adoption announcement (company adding ISO 27001 cert, etc.)
- Audit cycle within 90 days
- New regulatory mandate finalized in their vertical
- Cyber insurance application open

**Onyxia's response:**
> Onyxia maps your controls to whichever framework the auditor is asking for, so one control set produces six framework views.

**Proof:**
- Framework Compliance Dashboard feature: NIST CSF 2.0, HIPAA, HITRUST, FedRamp, SOC 2, PCI, custom (ISO). One-click overlay.
- "We pull from the stack you already have. Not another data entry job." — GRC persona language.

**Verticals where sharpest:** Healthcare, higher ed, life sciences, federal contracting, regulated finserv.

### 6. We passed the audit in March, but I cannot tell you we are still passing today

**Plain English:** Your last audit was eight months ago. Since then, half your engineers have rotated. Three new tools went live. Two integrations broke and nobody noticed. Are you still passing the controls you passed in March? Today you do not know. The board assumes you do.

**Who feels it most:**
- Mature CISOs (24+ months in role)
- Audit committee chairs
- Cyber insurers (reading customer attestations)
- CFOs renewing E&O / D&O / cyber policies

**Triggers:**
- Between-audit periods (the boring 9 months nobody reports on)
- Posture-changing event: org change, M&A, geographic expansion
- Cyber insurance renewal
- Recent breach in adjacent sub-vertical (raises board scrutiny)

**Onyxia's response:**
> Onyxia checks every day whether your controls are still doing what they were doing on audit day.

**Proof:**
- CPI Library feature: pre-built indicators with formulas, SLAs, historical trend
- Benchmarking feature: compare against industry SLA averages
- Continuous Threat Exposure Management positioning

**Verticals where sharpest:** Mature regulated industries (banking, insurance, healthcare hospitals, defense).

### 8. The recipe IS the company; lose it and we are done

**Plain English:** The intellectual property at your company is not interchangeable with anyone else's. The gene therapy protocols. The trading algorithms. The chip designs. The drug pipeline. The defense weapons platform. Lose it to a hacker and there is no rebuild. Standard security treats this asset class the same as everyone else's email. It is not the same.

**Who feels it most:**
- Biotech / pharma CISOs
- Defense prime CISOs (CUI environments)
- Hedge fund CISOs (algorithms)
- Semiconductor CISOs (designs, fab data)
- Specialty manufacturing (chemistry, materials)

**Triggers:**
- Pre-IPO filing (S-1)
- New product launch in regulated category
- Recent IP-theft news in adjacent sector
- M&A activity (acquirer or target)
- New patent filing surge
- New CISO at IP-sensitive company

**Onyxia's response:**
> Onyxia tracks whether the controls protecting your most critical IP are working every day, separate from the rest of your stack.

**Proof:**
- Stack Map asset-type axis includes Data and Applications
- CPI Library customizable to IP-specific controls
- Asset Coverage Analyzer maps tool coverage per asset

**Verticals where sharpest:** Biotech (especially gene therapy / cell therapy), defense primes, semiconductor, hedge funds.

- **Don't stack.** Pick ONE spine pain. The cameo regulation gets one sentence at most. Other pains get zero mentions.
- **If the matrix says #X → #Y**, lead with #X if the contact-specific signal supports it; fall back to #Y if not.
