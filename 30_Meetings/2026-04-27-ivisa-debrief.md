## What happened

- **Pre-call (first ~2 min, Onyxia-internal):** Miles tested Granola's note-taking capability with Sivan and Brad while waiting for Susan to join. Sivan confirmed she prefers no bot joining the call (guest-friction objection from a prior Vadim customer call).

- **Hockey banter (3-4 min):** Sivan opened on Susan's Dallas Stars hat. Susan, Sivan, and Brad are all hockey fans. Extended small talk about playoff matchups (Stars vs. Minnesota; Boston Bruins getting crushed). This was rapport-building. Sivan drove it intentionally — she said it's rare to talk to a security leader who follows hockey and she would have "another calling" in that world.
- **Miles asked what stood out from Susan's prior research on Onyxia (~15:10 ET).** Susan responded by noting iVisa's Google presence, mentioned Onyxia's logo (joked about the dragon visual vs. competitors), and then pivoted into: "I'm not really exploring a lot of new tools right now. But I'm more like, taking a look at what people are doing."
- **Sivan ran a live platform demo (~15:12–15:25 ET).** Walked through: Stack Map / asset coverage, risk scoring against frameworks (NIST, ISO 27001, SOC 2), customizable dashboards, AI agent for cross-domain data correlation (example: "give me a list of devices using non-compliant configurations"), report generation with downloadable output, and Rapid7 deep-link evidence trail.
- **Susan engaged actively during demo.** She asked pointed questions about: how Onyxia is the "source of truth vs. source of record" for tool data, how evidence is sourced for auditors, how the risk scoring handles super-users vs. general users, and what happens when environments branch out beyond Google Workspace.

- **Miles wrapped at ~15:28 ET.** No specific next step committed. Susan said she'll "keep an eye on" Onyxia and if she has questions she'll email or LinkedIn.
- **Sivan closed warmly.** Susan said she's happy to be a peer referral point for practitioners who come to her with tool questions. The meeting ended on good terms.
